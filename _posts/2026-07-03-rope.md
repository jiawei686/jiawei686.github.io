**Paper:** Su, J., et al., *RoFormer: Enhanced Transformer with Rotary Position Embedding*, 2021. [arXiv:2104.09864](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.09864)

Position encoding is the quiet mechanism that tells a Transformer what order means. Absolute encodings, the original sine/cosine and learned vectors, do the job but they hide relative position. Relative position is what language actually cares about, since a word's meaning leans on its neighbors rather than on whether it's token 42 or 420. RoPE fixes this with a trick I still think is elegant: it rotates the query and key vectors by an angle tied to their position, so the attention dot product ends up depending only on the distance between two positions. It's the default now in LLaMA, Mistral, Qwen, DeepSeek, and most open-weight models. Read a modern model card and you're looking at RoPE.

## The move: rotate instead of add

For a pair of dimensions $(2i, 2i+1)$ of a vector at position $m$, RoPE applies a rotation by angle $m\theta_i$ with $\theta_i = 10000^{-2i/d}$:

$$
R_m =
\begin{bmatrix}
\cos m\theta_i & -\sin m\theta_i \\
\sin m\theta_i & \cos m\theta_i
\end{bmatrix}, \qquad
f(q, m) = R_m\, q
$$

Apply the same to the key: $\tilde{k}_n = R_n\, k$. The attention score between positions $m$ and $n$ becomes:

$$
\tilde{q}_m^\top \tilde{k}_n
= q^\top R_{m-n}\, k
$$

The property that matters: the score depends on $m$ and $n$ **only through their difference $m-n$**. Relative position drops out of the algebra for free, while the absolute position stays baked into each rotated vector.

## Extending past the training length

Because positions enter as rotations, you can stretch to longer contexts than you trained on by adjusting the base (NTK-aware scaling, used by LLaMA 2/3 and others) instead of re-training. This is why "bump context to 32k/128k" is usually a RoPE-base tweak, not a fresh pretrain.

## Results, briefly

- Matches or beats absolute and relative positional embeddings on BERT-style and autoregressive tasks.
- Enables clean relative-position attention without extra bias terms.
- Became the de-facto standard for long-context open models.

## The part that matters

Every "128k context" label on an open model is RoPE plus its NTK variants letting it stretch without a from-scratch retrain. Read it next to the Transformer post: RoPE is the position-encoding answer to "how does attention know word order?" My one gripe is that the relative-position story is clean on paper, but the moment you start bending the base for extrapolation you trade off how well the model reasons about the positions it actually saw. That tension is still unsolved.
